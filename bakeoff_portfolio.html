Key insights from the bakeoff
Why Lasso was chosen as the project model
The assignment focused on L1 regularization and its connection to Dropout in deep learning. Lasso uniquely eliminates features entirely — Ridge does not. This makes Lasso a regression model and a feature selector in one, which was the core learning objective.
Why XGBoost scores higher (83.75% vs 63.74%)
California housing prices are non-linear. XGBoost captures complex interactions between income, location, and density that a linear model structurally cannot. The ~20% gap quantifies the cost of linearity constraints, not a failure of Lasso itself.
When to choose Lasso over tree-based models
Lasso is the right choice when interpretability matters — in regulated industries like banking or healthcare where you need to explain exactly which features drove a prediction. XGBoost gives you better numbers; Lasso gives you answers.
The positive constraint experiment (V14)
Forcing all coefficients non-negative dropped Test R² from 63.74% to 56.51%. This proved that some features — like latitude — have genuinely negative relationships with house prices. Mathematical constraints must respect the data's reality.
